How to choose the best VPS providers?

Okay, so now you know how the best VPS providers should be like. But, how do you choose one which suits your need best? Here’s how:

  • Managed vs. Unmanaged hosts:Can you manage your VPS server yourself? If you can’t, go with a managed host. These cost more but you don’t need to worry about the background technicalities.
  • Root access: Not all VPS hosts provide root access. Those which don’t, aren’t necessarily the “bad” choices. A number of managed hosts refrain from offering you root access, lest you mess something up.
  • cPanel: Do you require cPanel? I personally feel it makes things a lot easier. However, it’ll cost a couple dollar extra. Although, you can go without it as well.
  • OS-choices: OS-choices
  • Automated backups: I feel it’s best to go with VPS hosts which offer free automated backups.
  • Other freebies: Some best VPS hosting companies will also offer free website builders, free migrations, SSL certificates, CDN and even .com domains; while others won’t. Obviously, those which do are the better choices in most cases.
  • Renewal costs: Verify this during signup that your VPS server cost wouldn’t increase exponentially when you renew your server. Most companies offer cheap onboarding, but demand a fortune on renewal. Namecheap is a company which boasts “same price renewals”.
